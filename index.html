Dhemaji District Floods 2023: A Regional Crisis

Understanding the devastating impact on Dhemaji district, one of the worst-affected areas in Assam's 2023 flooding disaster

The Unfolding Disaster

The 2023 floods in Dhemaji district marked one of the most severe localized climate disasters in northeastern India's recent history. Beginning in May and persisting through August, unprecedented rainfall from the upper Brahmaputra catchment and overflowing tributaries including the Jia Bharali, Subansiri, and Ranganadi rivers devastated this border district.

Located in the upper reaches of Assam along the Arunachal Pradesh border, Dhemaji became the epicenter of our flood dynamics study, where advanced satellite radar technology revealed the complete transformation of the region's hydrological landscape and provided crucial insights into disaster progression in vulnerable riverine communities.

The Need for Advanced Monitoring

Traditional flood monitoring systems failed to capture the full scale and progression of the 2023 disaster. This crisis highlighted the critical need for advanced remote sensing technologies like SAR to provide real-time, weather-independent flood mapping and early warning systems.

Understanding SAR Technology and Data Processing

üõ∞Ô∏è

SAR Data Acquisition

Multi-temporal SAR data collection using C-band and L-band frequencies with different polarizations to capture surface characteristics across varying penetration depths.

  • Sentinel-1 C-band SAR imagery
  • Multi-polarization analysis (VV, VH, HH, HV)
  • Temporal resolution: 12-day revisit cycle
  • Spatial resolution: 10m ground range
üî¨

Image Processing Pipeline

Advanced SAR processing techniques including speckle filtering, geometric correction, and radiometric calibration to ensure data quality and accuracy.

  • Speckle noise reduction
  • Geometric terrain correction
  • Radiometric calibration to sigma-nought
  • Multi-temporal image registration
üìä

Change Detection Analysis

Quantitative analysis of backscatter coefficient changes to identify flood extent, vegetation stress, and recovery patterns across the study period.

  • Backscatter threshold analysis
  • Coherence change detection
  • Polarimetric decomposition
  • Statistical significance testing

SAR Signature Interpretation

Water Bodies

Dark areas indicate smooth water surfaces that reflect radar signals away from the sensor (low backscatter).

Vegetation

Gray areas show vegetation that scatters radar signals in multiple directions (moderate backscatter).

Built Areas

Bright areas indicate structures that strongly reflect radar signals back to the sensor (high backscatter).

Technical Innovations

Multi-frequency Analysis: C-band and L-band integration provided comprehensive surface and subsurface characterization.
Polarimetric Decomposition: Freeman-Durden decomposition revealed distinct scattering mechanisms in flood-affected areas.
Coherence Mapping: Interferometric coherence analysis detected subtle changes in surface roughness and vegetation structure.

Looking Forward: SAR for Climate Resilience
